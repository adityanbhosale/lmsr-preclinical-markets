## Why LS-LMSR over a CFMM

Constant-function market makers (Uniswap-class CFMMs) work for assets with two-sided order flow and roughly known fundamental value. Event contracts have neither property: flow is one-sided until resolution approaches, and the fundamental value is precisely what the market is supposed to discover. A CFMM in this setting either burns through LP capital via adverse selection (Loss-versus-Rebalancing in Milionis et al. 2022) or quotes uselessly wide spreads.

LS-LMSR resolves this by making the liquidity parameter `b` a function of cumulative quantity. The market starts thin (low `b`, prices respond strongly to small trades — rewards early traders for moving consensus) and deepens as activity accumulates (high `b`, later trades move prices less — protects against late-stage manipulation).

## ABMM seeding

The Automated Bioactivity Market Maker (ABMM) seeds each market with an initial position derived from a confidence-weighted prior. The ABMM retreats as credentialed trading volume accumulates — its influence decays exponentially with cumulative LDI.

This is the central distinctive mechanism in the system. Derivation, justification, and the open theoretical question on distortion bounds are documented in [Mechanism Design → Retreat Function](/mechanism/retreat-function).
