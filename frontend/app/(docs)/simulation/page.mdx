## Sweep results (H1 + H2)

I ran two sweeps to validate the interactive mechanism above against the full
parameter space. **H2** (1,500 runs) measures convergence to true probability
under varying agent populations; **H1** (2,400 runs) measures retreat-function
robustness under coordinated adversarial attacks.

### H2 — convergence to true probability

A representative run with a 40/40/20 credentialed/noise/momentum mix and a
starting prior 35 percentage points away from the true probability.

/>

Across 1,500 runs spanning five agent mixes, five starting priors, and three
true probabilities, convergence rate scales sharply with credentialed share:

<img
  src="/sim/h2_heatmap_mix_prior.png"
  alt="H2: convergence rate by credentialed share × starting prior"
  className="w-full rounded border border-border my-4"
/>

Convergence to within ε=0.03 of true probability is ≥97% reliable when
credentialed agents are ≥40% of the population. In that regime, median
convergence is 312 ticks (IQR 193–482).

### H1 — retreat under attack

A representative run with a 25%-liquidity adversarial buy at tick 300,
exponential retreat τ=1.5. The attack pushes price toward 1.0, retreat
engages, and price drifts back toward `true_prob = 0.65` over the next
500 ticks.

<img
  src="/sim/h1_trajectory_example.png"
  alt="H1 single-run trajectory: 25% YES-attack at tick 300, exponential τ=1.5"
  className="w-full rounded border border-border my-4"
/>

Across 2,400 runs spanning three decay shapes, four attack magnitudes,
both attack directions, and 25 seeds, recovery degrades with magnitude
but the decay shape matters at every level:

<img
  src="/sim/h1_recovery_by_shape.png"
  alt="H1: median recovery fraction by decay shape and attack magnitude"
  className="w-full rounded border border-border my-4"
/>

The retreat function reverses ~80–90% of attack displacement at 10% liquidity
attacks, falling to ~25–50% at 50% attacks. **Polynomial decay dominates
exponential and step at every magnitude tested.**
