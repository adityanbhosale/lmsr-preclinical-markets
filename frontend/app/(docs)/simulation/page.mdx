# Simulation

The dual-layer mechanism is best understood by watching it move. The interactive panel
below runs the same LS-LMSR + agent code that produced the H1/H2 sweep results — ported
from Python to TypeScript and validated to within stochastic variance.

## Interactive mechanism

<InteractiveL2Sim />

Adjust the agent population and watch the price converge toward `true_prob`. The dashed
gray line is the retreat factor — it stays at 1.0 until human volume crosses the threshold,
then decays according to the chosen shape. Fire an adversarial attack to see whether the
ABMM retreat reverses the displacement.

## Sweep results (H1 + H2)

Two batched sweeps validate the interactive mechanism above against the full
parameter space. **H2** (1,500 runs) measures convergence to true probability
under varying agent populations; **H1** (2,400 runs) measures retreat-function
robustness under coordinated adversarial attacks.

<img
  src="/sim/h2_heatmap_mix_prior.png"
  alt="H2: convergence rate by credentialed share × starting prior"
  className="w-full rounded border border-border my-4"
/>

Convergence to within ε=0.03 of true probability is ≥97% reliable when
credentialed agents are ≥40% of the population. In that regime, median
convergence is 312 ticks (IQR 193–482).

<img
  src="/sim/h1_recovery_by_shape.png"
  alt="H1: median recovery fraction by decay shape and attack magnitude"
  className="w-full rounded border border-border my-4"
/>

The retreat function reverses ~80–90% of attack displacement at 10% liquidity
attacks, falling to ~25–50% at 50% attacks. Polynomial decay dominates
exponential and step at every magnitude tested — a real architectural
recommendation, not just a yes/no.
